Milestones Along the Way

  • 1958

    Maria Teresa de Filippis becomes the first woman to race in F1

  • 1981

    Michèle Mouton became the first woman to win a World Rally Championship

  • 2008

    Danica Patrick won the Indy Japan 300, making her the first woman to win an IndyCar race

  • 2012

    Susie Wolff became the first woman in over 20 years to take part in an official F1 race weekend

  • 2023

    F1 Academy launched, women-only racing series aimed at preparing young female drivers for higher levels like F3, F2, and eventually F1.

Breakthrough in Action

These women are not just in the sport — they’re shaping it. From the garage to the grid to your feed, they’re changing what’s possible in motorsport.

Hannah Schmitz

As Red Bull’s Principal Strategy Engineer, Hannah has called race-winning plays under pressure. Her calm leadership is redefining what power looks like on the pit wall.

Abbi Pulling

After claiming the 2024 F1 Academy title, Abbi is proving that with the right platform, women can dominate the track and inspire the next generation.

Lizzie Mackintosh

As a journalist and digital creator, Lizzie is bringing fans behind the scenes and showing that motorsport media has room for new voices — and new perspectives.
