The Barriers

Before women can compete at the highest levels, they first have to *get* there — and the road is anything but smooth. These are the barriers holding many back.

Explore the Gaps

What’s holding women back

From access to attitude, the playing field is far from level. These are the core barriers shaping women’s journey in motorsport today.

Early Access

Most drivers start karting by age 6–8. But fewer girls get access to tracks, gear, or encouragement early enough to build competitive skills.

Funding & Support

Motorsport is expensive — and male drivers often attract more sponsorship, team backing, and financial support from the start.

Media & Representation

Female talent receives a fraction of the coverage. That lack of visibility feeds the cycle: fewer fans, less funding, less opportunity.

Bias & Culture

From stereotypes to outright exclusion, women face subtle and overt bias that affects how teams, media, and fans perceive them.

Behind the Pit Wall

From race engineers to strategists, women are vastly underrepresented in technical and leadership roles — facing bias, gatekeeping, and a lack of visible pathways in the paddock.

More Than the Track

Barriers exist off the grid too. Whether it's a girl dreaming of working on the pit wall or a fan feeling excluded from the community, the challenges go beyond just driving. Representation matters everywhere — in garages, media rooms, and grandstands.

"There’s a whole generation of girls who don’t know they’re allowed to dream of F1. That has to change."

– Susie Wolff, F1 Academy Managing Director

But that’s changing

From grassroots programs to the rise of F1 Academy, momentum is building. The barriers are real — but so is the progress.
